Course Overview

  1. The Great American Novel?
  2. Lacking (Traditional|European) History
  3. Enlightenment Nation / Enlightenment Form
  1. The Great American
    • Probably heard this before?
    • What do you think it means?
    • Can you think of some examples?
      • Moby Dick
      • The Great Gatsby
  2. **Lacking (Traditional European) History**
    • Famous joke: England has houses older than our country.
    • But what does that mean? We don't have an appeal to an unbroken tradition tracing back to Rome or Greece or Vikings.
    • America is a nation cobbled together out of immigrants in an unfamiliar (to Europeans).
    • This, of course, denies / ignores the Native traditions in this country pre-colonization, but …
    • … that's sort of the point: while trying to write a universal myth of America, often very white, male oriented.
  3. Enlightenment Nation / Enlightenment Form
    • Despite all that baggage, America is also a nation created during and from Enlightenment principles
    • Enlightenment:
      • Democracy
      • B/c all humans are created equal with reason.
    • The novel, with it's focus on interiority enforces this idea.
    • Older literary forms (the epic, the play, parents reading The Bible out loud at home) are all oral.
    • Novel is a silent form. Meant to transmit author's rationality to reader's rationality.
    • So, in a sense, novel is natural for America's myth-making.
